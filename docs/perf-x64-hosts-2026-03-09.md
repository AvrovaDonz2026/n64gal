## Reading

1. `avx2` is strongly positive on both native x64 runners; there is no sign of the older `windows-x64 avx2 < scalar` failure mode in the current gate.
2. Linux x64 on `EPYC 7763` is currently faster than Windows x64 on `Xeon 8370C` for the same `avx2` workload shape, especially on `S10` and the two full-span textured kernels.
3. Even on the slower of the two x64 runners, the key textured full-span kernels are already sub-`1ms` p95, which supports the current choice of keeping kernel threshold in `soft/report-only` mode instead of escalating it to a blocking gate immediately.
4. `sprite_full_opaque` and `sprite_full_alpha180` remain the most important textured kernels to watch. They are no longer blockers, but they are still the clearest place where cross-runner x64 differences show up.

## Why This Matters

1. The project now records `host_cpu`, `requested_backend`, and `actual_backend`, so x64 performance discussions no longer need to guess whether a runner difference came from hardware or from a silent backend fallback.
2. This evidence gives us a stable baseline for later decisions:
   - whether to tighten x64 thresholds further
   - whether to keep `windows-x64 dirty S3` as report-only
   - whether a future `avx2` optimization is universally positive or only good on one x64 runner family
